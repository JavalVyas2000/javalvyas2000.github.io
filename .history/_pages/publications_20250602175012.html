LLM-based Fault Handling

This paper presents an agentic framework for autonomous fault handling in modular process plants using large language models (LLMs). By integrating LLM agents with a Digital Twin, the system diagnoses, and mitigates faults in a closed-loop manner.

Evaluation on a simulated chemical plant demonstrates the ability of LLMs to reason through faults and generate safe corrective actions, highlighting their potential for industrial autonomy.

LLM based Control Agent

This paper explores the use of large language models (LLMs) as autonomous control agents within an agentic framework for industrial automation. Through a case study on temperature regulation using CrewAI, the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of LLMs in controlling physical systems.

The proposed reprompting architecture improves agent performance, showcasing its potential for autonomous decision-making in industrial applications.

Feasibility Analysis using Linear Model Decision Trees

This paper presents an innovative approach to integrate plant scheduling with supply chain management under disruptions, leveraging machine learning surrogates to enhance decision-making.

By using a reactive supply chain model and linear decision tree surrogates, the method effectively addresses time-scale differences and ensures feasible scheduling solutions. A case study demonstrates its ability to maintain profitability while guaranteeing operational feasibility within reasonable computational times.

Using Machine Learning Surrogates for Produced Water Networks

This paper explores the integration of data-driven approaches for optimizing energy systems, focusing on using machine learning and predictive analytics for better performance and efficiency.

The proposed methodologies aim to enhance the management of energy production and consumption, leveraging real-time data to improve decision-making processes and reduce energy waste. The approach combines advanced modeling with practical applications, highlighting its effectiveness through case studies and experimental validation.

Optimization model and algorithms for the Unit Commitment problem

This paper presents a solution to the unit commitment problem by employing a decomposition method combined with the tight model from EGRET software, addressing its combinatorial complexity. Benchmark tests against four case studies demonstrate significant computational speedups without compromising solution quality. (Under review at ESCAPE-35)
