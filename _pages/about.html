Bio

I work at the intersection of machine learning, optimization, and structured reasoning. My research focuses on designing decision systems that combine statistical modeling with explicit structural constraints, ensuring reliability under complex and sequential settings.

During my PhD at Imperial College London, I developed multi-agent reasoning frameworks for structured planning tasks, analyzing convergence behavior and constraint enforcement in iterative decision processes. Alongside this, I build quantitative modeling systems that emphasize careful evaluation, robust backtesting, and constraint-aware optimization.

I am particularly interested in problems where correctness, feasibility, and stability matter — whether in large-scale ML systems, optimization-driven workflows, or quantitative decision pipelines.
